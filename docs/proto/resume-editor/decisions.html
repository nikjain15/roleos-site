CORE · Resume editor — design decisions

Status: APPROVED (2026-07-05) · PRD shipped: roleos-app PR #57 (stacked on #51) · prototype at /proto/resume-editor/ · EXTENDS the live slice-1/3 editor — the PRD diffs kept-vs-new so the builder extends, never rebuilds.

Confirmed intent

The flagship craft: your whole career retold for one role without ever becoming fiction. Opens as ONE clean, complete document — the tailored resume, readable as a recruiter would — with quiet dots marking changed lines; everything else lives behind lenses opened one at a time (what changed & why · compare with master · robot view), and RO orients you once in three lines. Every change explains itself in plain English at your chosen depth (concise/detailed toggle, remembered). We out-ATS the ATS tools honestly: real must-have coverage built only from things you did, gaps named, never faked. The truth flag stays from v1, resolvable in place. Your edits teach her voice; edits that beat the master trigger a propose-and-approve upgrade — the master never changes behind your back. Export unlocks when every line is grounded.

Locked decisions (incl. the three CORE questions)

#DecisionChoice
1World-class bar (CORE)Truth + taste + honestly out-ATS-ing the ATS tools (human combo). Beats Teal/Rezi because keyword coverage comes from real experience (their stuffed output dies in interviews); beats ChatGPT because she remembers and compounds. The robot view makes ATS strength a visible, honest capability.
2Visible vs invisible intelligence (CORE)Everything explained, in plain English, layered (human): concise one-liners by default, full reasoning on demand, a concise/detailed toggle persisted per user. Mechanical work (keyword placement, formatting) compressed to one summary line.
3Overridables (CORE)Everything editable, edits clear their own flags (you own your claims — kept from v1). Master upgrades: she proposes, you approve — the source of truth never mutates silently.
4The editor’s shapeRebuilt as a document editor showing the COMPLETE resume (all years, apples to apples) — human requirement: resumes decide selections, so the user must see the whole document, not fragments. Compare-with-master is a full-document side-by-side lens.
5Calm UX (standing rule, born here)Default = the artifact alone with quiet dots · lenses one at a time (Resume / What changed / Compare / Robot view) · once-only 3-line orientation · mobile: lenses as full screens.
5bThe change walker (feedback round 1)“Review 1-by-1” — every change as its own step: master’s version → her version in an editable box → the why → keep / save-my-edit / revert-to-master. Pause anytime, place held without nagging. Dots stay for spot-checks; the walker is the primary editing path.
6Comment systemTyped, not piled: blue why (pull-only) · amber truth flag (blocks export until resolved) · green suggestion (optional) · pencil user note (private). Actionables arrive via a one-at-a-time queue ordered by stakes (truth → suggestions); “later” always allowed, queue waits without nagging; resolved comments leave the document (history in the changes lens). Honest calm counts (“2 need your eyes” → “all clear”), never alarm badges. Generalizes to the cover-letter editor (feature 7).
7Robot view“How their parser reads this”: per-must-have coverage with three honest states (covered-from-real-work / adjacent-framed-honestly / missing-and-not-faked). Weak coverage → she offers fixes that use only real experience. No score theater.
8Export gateDOCX + PDF (kept); unlocks when every line is grounded. Keeping a flagged claim after her warning is allowed (your claim, your call) — noted in flag history.
9Voice learningEvery edit is a voice lesson (high-weight decision event); her future drafts converge on how you write. Named to the user (“learning it”).
