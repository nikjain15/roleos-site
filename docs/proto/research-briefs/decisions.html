CORE · Research briefs — design decisions

Status: APPROVED (2026-07-05) · PRD shipped: roleos-app PR #59 (stacked on #58) · prototype at /proto/research-briefs/ · EXTENDS X2 (corpus brief kept as fallback).

Confirmed intent

Turn the night-before hour of panicked googling into a two-minute read with an iceberg under it. The dossier is comprehensive but calm: her short version on top, full sections opening one at a time beneath. Maximum flexibility in the ask-anything bar: name a source or let her pick (she says which and why); answers land in the dossier permanently, receipts attached. Interviewers get public-professional research only — what they chose to publish — never scraped, never personal; the user can add what they know (recruiter email, a friend’s tip). Low-profile people get honesty, not invention. Receipts are load-bearing everywhere; “what I don’t know” is said out loud. Delivery is ambient: full brief T-24h with the prep, 30-second refresher the morning of, on-demand always.

Locked decisions (incl. the three CORE questions)

#DecisionChoice
1World-class bar (CORE)Comprehensive dossier + interactive research (human): calm 2-minute layer on top (calm-UX rule), full sections beneath, and an ask-anything bar — user names the source or RO picks and says why. Maximum flexibility over curated minimalism.
2Interviewer research (THE deferred X2 decision)Public-professional only + user uploads (human): what the person chose to publish (talks, posts, papers, company bio, public product work) — framed as “what they care about professionally.” NO LinkedIn scraping (consistent with the X6 ruling: last, prefer never), nothing personal. Users can paste/upload what they know (recruiter emails, friends’ tips) — consent-clean color. Low-profile people → honest thinness + role-based inference, labeled.
3Visible intelligence (CORE)Synthesis with visible receipts: every claim tap-to-source; “what I don’t know” section says the quiet part out loud (“anyone who claims to know is guessing”). One wrong claim repeated in an interview is catastrophic — receipts are load-bearing.
4DeliveryT-24h full brief with the prep + 30-second morning refresher (“three things walking in”). Calendar-aware when connected; tracker-stage-triggered otherwise; on-demand always.
5Overridables (CORE)Source choice per ask · every dossier answer editable/removable · uploads deletable · interviewer research per-person opt-out (“don’t research people for this one”).

Infrastructure flag (the slice’s one big decision)

Live external researchX2 is corpus-only today. The dossier depth + ask-bar require flag-gated live fetching (egress allowlist + source review — the roadmap’s anticipated v2). Corpus brief is the graceful fallback until flipped. Every live answer carries provenance; fetch costs metered.
