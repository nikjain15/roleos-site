## What the role involves

**Surface:** Executive Protection (EP) program within Global Safety, Intelligence, and Security (GSIS) team

**Business model:** internal corporate function (non-revenue); security operations enablement

**Team size:** unclear

**Cross-functional partners:** Administrative Business Partners (ABPs), Global Travel, Legal, Tax compliance, GSIS Intelligence team, Global Security Operations Center (GSOC), Corporate Travel, PR/Communications, IT/InfoSec

**Primary metrics (inferred):** KPIs for program readiness and response times; Fiscal health / budget adherence for protective services; Coverage windows and staffing capacity utilization
**Core responsibilities:**

- Define, structure, and scale the executive protection program into a proactive corporate function that grows with Anthropic
- Translate threat intelligence assessments from the GSIS Intelligence team into concrete, data-backed operational capacity plans — staffing, physical assets, coverage windows
- Establish and track KPIs and operational metrics for program readiness, response times, and fiscal health
- Develop and maintain repeatable domestic and international travel operational frameworks, including secure-transport playbooks and post-mortems
- Partner with the Global Security Operations Center (GSOC) to design communication protocols and alert pipelines for protected individuals
- Facilitate selection, acquisition, and maintenance of specialized equipment, vehicles, travel-security applications, and communications technology
- Run sourcing, RFP processes, onboarding, and ongoing management of global third-party physical security, secure transport, and intelligence vendors
- Own the protective services budget, including in-house vs. contract cost-benefit analyses and quarterly/annual planning reviews
- Integrate protective operations into daily corporate workflows by partnering with ABPs, Corporate Travel, Legal, PR/Communications, and IT/InfoSec
- Partner with legal counsel on compliance with state licensing laws, corporate insurance policies, and privacy mandates
