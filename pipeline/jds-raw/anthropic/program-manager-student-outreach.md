# Program Manager, Student Outreach — Anthropic

## About Anthropic

Anthropic’s mission is to create reliable, interpretable, and steerable AI systems. We want AI to be safe and beneficial for our users and for society as a whole. Our team is a quickly growing group of committed researchers, engineers, policy experts, and business leaders working together to build beneficial AI systems.

## About the role

Anthropic is seeking a Program Manager to own and drive our student outreach strategy, establishing Claude as the AI platform of choice for the next generation of builders, researchers, and creators at universities worldwide. 

You'll lead flagship programs including the Claude Campus Ambassador Program and Claude Builder Clubs, while developing new initiatives that embody Anthropic's mission of helping humanity navigate the AI transition.

 This is a high-ownership role where you'll define the vision, build the infrastructure, and personally drive execution for how Anthropic engages with student communities globally. Beyond keeping programs running smoothly, you'll make real strategic decisions and own the student narrative company-wide. 

We're looking for someone who thrives with significant autonomy, can operate effectively with ambiguity, and has the judgment to make consequential calls about where we invest our resources.

## Responsibilities

- Lead the Claude Campus Ambassador Program, recruiting, training, and supporting student ambassadors at universities worldwide

- Build and scale Claude Builder Clubs to foster student communities around AI development

- Design and execute brand activations and event programming tailored for student audiences

- Develop overall strategy and positioning for Claude with the student persona

- Manage student hackathon partnerships at scale, including sponsorships, judging, and mentorship programs

- Lead outreach efforts to grow Claude awareness and usage on university campuses globally

- Develop new programs that apply Anthropic's mission of helping humanity through the AI transition to the student audience

- Collaborate with product, sales, developer relations, marketing and communications teams to ensure student programs align with broader company goals

- Track and report on program metrics, continuously optimizing for impact and scale

## Minimum Qualifications

- Proven track record of building and scaling ambassador or community programs in education or edtech space

- Track record of making strategic decisions with incomplete information and delivering results

- Experience operating autonomously and driving outcomes without heavy oversight

- Strong communication skills—you'll represent Anthropic to university leadership, partners, and across internal teams

- Proven ability to manage complex, multi-stakeholder initiatives while keeping execution sharp

- Comfort with travel for campus events and hackathons (estimated 5-10%)

## Preferred Qualifications

- 5+ years of experience in program management, developer relations, community building, or campus engagement, with increasing scope and ownership over time

- Experience building campus or student programs at a major technology company

- Background in hackathon organization or developer community ecosystems

- Technical fluency or hands-on experience with AI/ML

- International program management experience across multiple regions

- Experience with brand strategy or experiential marketing

## How we're different

We believe that the highest-impact AI research will be big science. At Anthropic we work as a single cohesive team on just a few large-scale research efforts. And we value impact — advancing our long-term goals of steerable, trustworthy AI — rather than work on smaller and more specific puzzles. We view AI research as an empirical science, which has as much in common with physics and biology as with traditional efforts in computer science. We're an extremely collaborative group, and we host frequent research discussions to ensure that we are pursuing the highest-impact work at any given time. As such, we greatly value communication skills.

The easiest way to understand our research directions is to read our recent research. This research continues many of the directions our team worked on prior to Anthropic, including: GPT-3, Circuit-Based Interpretability, Multimodal Neurons, Scaling Laws, AI & Compute, Concrete Problems in AI Safety, and Learning from Human Preferences.
