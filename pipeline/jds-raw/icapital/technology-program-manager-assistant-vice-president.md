# Technology Program Manager - Assistant Vice President — iCapital

## About the Role

iCapital is seeking a Technology Program Manager to partner closely with Engineering / Technology leadership to deliver complex, high-impact initiatives across the firm’s most critical technology priorities.

This role sits at the intersection of strategy and execution — bringing best-in-class program management practices to large-scale, cross-functional initiatives. The team operates in an internal consulting capacity, driving clarity, alignment, and disciplined execution across multi-team programs.

You will engage across a diverse portfolio including greenfield software development, enterprise vendor implementations, cloud and infrastructure initiatives, data & analytics and machine learning efforts, and broader technology innovation programs.

As iCapital continues to scale, we are increasingly leveraging AI-enabled tools and automation to enhance engineering productivity and program transparency. This role requires comfort operating in an AI-augmented environment — identifying opportunities to use emerging technologies to improve planning, reporting, decision-making, and cross-team coordination.

This is a leadership and execution role focused on orchestration, visibility, and delivery excellence — it does not involve hands-on coding or testing.

 

## Key Responsibilities

- Lead end-to-end delivery of complex, cross-functional technology programs spanning multiple engineering teams

- Partner with Engineering to define scope, roadmap, milestones, and measurable outcomes

- Establish clear governance, reporting, and risk management practices to ensure transparency and accountability

- Communicate program status, risks, dependencies, and mitigation strategies to senior stakeholders

- Collaborate with Technology leadership to shape forward-looking strategy and execution frameworks

- Drive continuous improvement initiatives to enhance delivery effectiveness across the broader Technology organization

- Bring structure and clarity to ambiguous or evolving initiatives

 

## Qualifications

- 5–10 years of relevant experience

- Experience in at least two of the following:

- Technical Program Management

- Financial services industry

- Technology or financial services consulting

- Strong understanding of software development lifecycle (SDLC) methodologies

- Familiarity with relational database concepts and data modeling

- Exposure to cloud technologies (e.g., AWS)

- Experience with project management tools such as JIRA, Monday.com, or similar platforms

- Bachelor’s degree in Engineering, Computer Science, Business Administration, or related field preferred

- PMP certification a plus

 

## Core Competencies

- Ability to influence stakeholders and build consensus across diverse viewpoints

- Strategic thinker who can connect big-picture objectives to detailed execution plans

- Comfortable navigating complexity and ambiguity

- Exceptional organizational skills and attention to detail

- Strong interpersonal skills with a collaborative, team-oriented mindset

- Excellent verbal and written communication skills

- Ability to operate effectively both independently and within cross-functional teams

 

Employees in this role will work fully remote. Every department has different needs, and some positions will be designated in-office jobs, based on their function.
