## 💻 Role
The Logistics Strategy & Operations team is responsible for the performance, reliability, and pricing of shipping on Whatnot. In this role, you will:

- Help define the operating model and strategy to enable scalable, high-quality logistics across all of Whatnot's markets

- Own carrier performance and pricing health, monitoring key metrics, managing rate structures, and driving accountability with external carrier partners

- Lead operational response to shipping disruptions, building scalable processes and playbooks that ensure fast, reliable resolution for buyers and sellers

- Inform the product and technology roadmap, translating operational pain points into prioritized requirements for Product and Engineering

- Shape carrier network strategy, evaluating coverage gaps, managing vendor relationships, and recommending updates as Whatnot scales into new categories and geographies

- Partner cross-functionally with BD, Finance, Product, and Operations as a strategic thought partner, providing data-driven recommendations to inform shipping decisions

Team members in this role are required to be within commuting distance of our New York, San Francisco, Phoenix, or Los Angeles hubs.

## 👋 You
Curious about who thrives at Whatnot? We’ve found that low ego, a growth mindset, and leaning into action and high impact goes a long way here. As our next Logistics Strategy & Operations team member, you should have 8–10 years of experience in strategy and operations roles (e.g., consulting, finance, logistics, supply chain, carrier operations), plus:

- Demonstrated success developing and executing strategies that drive scalable, high-quality operations. Direct experience with logistics operations and multi-market shipping networks is a strong plus.

- Ability to identify operational inefficiencies and design scalable processes, systems, and operating models. You default to repeatable systems over one-off solutions.

- Strong analytical judgment, with the ability to synthesize quantitative and qualitative insights into clear recommendations and decisions. Proficiency in SQL or analytics platforms (e.g., Looker, Sigma, Hex).

- Excellent communication and stakeholder management skills, with the ability to influence across Product, Engineering, Operations, and senior leadership.

- Comfort with ambiguity and speed. You've operated in high-growth environments where the playbook didn't exist yet, and you wrote it anyway.
